Your have idea for a product that might change the world. Maybe even several ideas. Or several dozen. You are working on some of those ideas in the evenings, on one or two even 'seriously'. Yet you never ask anyone for help, and very rarely tell anyone about your ideas. Why? Maybe you think that it is pointless, they either will not understand, or they will and will then either steal the idea, or will want to build it together with you.

And if later possibility, then suddenly you will be in the land of 'lets build it together and split spoils evenly.' Regardless of invested effort, regardless of who came up with the idea. And it is a commitment from you as well. It is serious stuff. And you back off and leave your ideas in a drawer. Lose-Lose situation for all potentially involved persons.

And all that will change with re-garage. Idea is to use agile principles for investing time and effort for you and your friends. You create idea, describe it in a private area of re-garage, set up some inital goals and some tasks that need to be done to reach those goals. Goals could be to do inital market research, build a product prototype, or anything else you feel is needed to launch the product. For each goal you can assign percentage of 'spoils', i.e. equity in future company.

For each goal there are number of tasks to be performed - and in this stage you invite friends with relevant skills, experience, time and willingess to participate. You sit down and groom the tasks until you all agree that this is what needs to be done to finish the goal. And then you estimate complexity of each task in a scrum way, with planning poker and story points. And then everyone picks the task and starts doing it.

Of course different people have different time available, have different experience and skill, so they finish their tasks in different time and end up having done different amount of story points. Also, new tasks come in, as new stuff is found out and goal is defined more precisely. So it can take several iterations to finish the goal. And at the end, all the story points spent on this goal are summed together, and each participant gets proportional percentage of 'spoils' attached to this goal.

Then next goal comes up and is done in similar fashion, but not necessarely the same people. Goals can also be fulfilled in parallel. And at the end project is launched and everybody involved can feel fairly compensated for their efforts, with no past, present and future grudges.

And yes - you came up with the idea, so you get say extra 5% of potential corporation that grows out of it.

Target of this fundraising campaign is to fund work of one person to work on re-garage for three months, cover costs of server for first year of operations as well as costs for some help from designer to finish the product.
